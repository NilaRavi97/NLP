The debate on privacy in marketplaces compares decentralized systems like Open Bazaar, which resist government interference but risk user deanonymization, with centralized alternatives that are vulnerable to attacks but can react swiftly to threats like Sybil attacks. While both can use encryption and cryptocurrency, centralized marketplaces have competitive incentives for administrative honesty and can host data more securely, whereas decentralized ones have distributed hosting, offering redundancy but not always superior privacy.
