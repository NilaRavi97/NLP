In the debate on free press in democracy, proponents assert it's vital for an informed public, accountability, and transparency, fostering democratic values. Critics believe media competition can lead to sensationalism and biased reporting due to funding sources. Despite concerns about the spread of misinformation, the consensus is the press must exercise freedom responsibly. Although limitations exist, a free press is necessary for democracy. Press serving owner interests may compromise true freedom, yet an informed public remains central to democratic ideals.
