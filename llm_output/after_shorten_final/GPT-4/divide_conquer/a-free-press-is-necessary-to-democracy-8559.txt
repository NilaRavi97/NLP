During the debate, the necessity of a free press in democracies for accountability and informed voting was highlighted, yet concerns about sensationalism, bias, and misinformation were noted. Participants discussed the balance between journalistic regulation and self-correction. Influence from powerful media owners and the risk of spreading harmful content were challenges identified. Although some debaters questioned the indispensability of press freedom, citing that educated voters are not a prerequisite for democracy, others insisted on the vital role of an unbiased press in fostering rational decision-making during elections.
