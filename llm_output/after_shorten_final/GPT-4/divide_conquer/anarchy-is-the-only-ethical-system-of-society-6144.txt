During a debate on the government's role, advocates underlined its importance in ensuring order and offering services, while recognizing potential tyrannical dangers. The conversation balanced personal freedoms against societal responsibilities, particularly in healthcare, and grappled with defining personal welfare. The legitimacy of coercive measures in health emergencies, like enforcing quarantines, was justified for public safety despite pacifist opposition. Alternately, some argued that an anarchist society with decentralized laws could achieve stability without central authority.
