The debate discussed automation's effect on employment and appropriate policy responses, including limited government and lower taxation, some equating the latter with theft. The Non-Aggression Principle's application to non-violent crime was debated, along with concerns about limited government's ability to tackle complex crimes and prevent market-driven oppression. The potential role of civil courts in addressing contracts and monopolies was considered, amidst doubts about the effectiveness of minimal state structures for defense and law enforcement without reliance on tax revenues.
