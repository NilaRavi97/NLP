
Cats are preferred over dogs as pets due to their low maintenance and cost. They eat and drink less, don't need bathing or grooming, and don't require daily walks. Small dog breeds can be cheaper than larger dogs. Dogs are more playful, but some cat breeds are also playful. Cats are cleaner and spend less time grooming, while dogs are better companions due to their intelligence, bonding, and obedience. Cats are independent and don't rely on their owners as much as dogs do. They have a higher level of intelligence and lower shedding of fur. Dogs have been domesticated longer and are more tailored to human preferences, but cats are currently more popular. Dogs bond with humans stronger than cats, but cats still express affection and desire. Cats are self-cleaning, but still need occasional baths. Dogs have been loyal to humans for thousands of years and have more open attributes like happiness. A study showed dogs playing with their owners had higher levels of oxytocin than cats. Finally, cats are typically less expensive to care for than dogs.
