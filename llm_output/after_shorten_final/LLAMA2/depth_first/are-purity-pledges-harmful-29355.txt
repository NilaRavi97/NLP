Encouraging young people to wait until marriage before having sex is a complex issue with both benefits and drawbacks. Some argue it leads to happier marriages, lower divorce rates, and better communication. Others claim it emphasizes developing other aspects of the relationship, like emotional intimacy. However, critics say forcing abstinence can lead to guilt, fear of sex, and a lack of support. Studies link purity pledges to a higher risk of pregnancy and STDs. Divorce rates aren't necessarily related to premarital sex, but rather commitment and communication. Ultimately, it depends on individual circumstances and values, and comprehensive sexual education, contraceptive options, and support from parents and society are crucial for informed decisions.
