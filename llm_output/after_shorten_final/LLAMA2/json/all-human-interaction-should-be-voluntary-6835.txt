
In this debate, participants discuss the role of government in enforcing the Non-Aggression Principle and preventing market failures. Some argue for limited government intervention, while others advocate for voluntaryism and the elimination of coercive taxation. Concerns are raised about the effectiveness of a small government in defending against foreign nations and addressing violent breaches of contracts. The discussion also touches on the potential benefits of a voluntary society, including minimizing government corruption and ending coercive taxing of citizens. Ultimately, the debate highlights the tension between the desire for limited government intervention and the need for some form of collective defense and governance.
