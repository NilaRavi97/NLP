One side argues that the fees are necessary to compensate clubs for developing players, provide a means for corruption, and allow agents to profit without contributing to the sport. The opposing side argues that the fees are unethical as they put a price tag on humans, can lead to inflated egos and pressure on players, and widen the gap between wealthy and poor clubs. The debate also touches upon the potential negative effects of transfer fees on individual players and the sport overall.
