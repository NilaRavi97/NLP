Debate participants discussed the role and impact of unions, highlighting benefits such as equal pay, collective bargaining, and improved worker benefits and dignity. Critics argued that unions can stifle productivity, raise costs, and sometimes function with outdated confrontational attitudes towards management. Some debated whether unions become self-interested entities, while others defended their role in democratizing workplaces and preventing exploitation. Concerns were raised over union corruption and potential for violence, yet many defended unions as necessary for balancing power between employees and larger corporations and maintaining workplace safety. The discussion also touched on unions' role in the public sector, with opinions divided on their appropriateness in managing public resources.
