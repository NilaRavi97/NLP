The debate examines the role of real estate brokers in the age of online listings, scrutinizing their fees and fraud risks against their negotiation skills and ability to increase sale prices. The emergence of technology platforms like Zillow prompts questions about brokers' future relevance. Concerns include potential infrastructure issues and fears of a monopolistic industry, but the example of Netflix disrupting film rental markets without monopolization is used to argue that technology-led market changes need not create monopolies.
