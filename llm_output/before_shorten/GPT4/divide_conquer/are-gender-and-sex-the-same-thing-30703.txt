The debate highlights the distinction between biological sex and gender identity, which is essential for transgender and intersex rights, scientific clarity, and addressing discrimination. Critics maintain their inseparability, while feminists caution against neglecting social influences on biology. With 0.6% of Americans identifying as transgender, recognizing the practicality of differentiating the two concepts is important. Sex is a biological characteristic, determined by genetic and physical factors, whereas gender is a social construct, shaped by cultural and historical context, and varies in expression across time and societies.
