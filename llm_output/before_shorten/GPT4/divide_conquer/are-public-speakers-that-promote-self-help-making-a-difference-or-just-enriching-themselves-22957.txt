The debate on self-help speakers evaluates their motivational value against their practical effectiveness. Supporters argue they inspire personal growth, as evidenced by paid attendance at events. Critics argue that the industry is profitable without offering concrete advice, with success being non-replicable due to individual differences. Concerns also pertain to the vague advice and potential placebo effects. While the industry's longevity suggests some merit, there's debate over whether vulnerable individuals might be misled by overly optimistic claims, risking financial loss in search of improvement.
