A computer with CAD software, an inexpensive 3D printer, and a cheap block of plastic, is able to create a working firearm. 3D printed plastic guns don't even work. but, Access to blueprints for early generations of 3D-printed firearms allows for crowd-sourced knowledge to develop and accelerate the improvement of prototypes. Removing the blueprint from major distributors such as gun rights organisations could substantially limit the supply of such weapons.
