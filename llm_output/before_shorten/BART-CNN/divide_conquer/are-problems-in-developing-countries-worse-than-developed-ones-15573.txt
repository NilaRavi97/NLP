For various structural reasons, governments in the developing world can not deal with problems in their states. Many basic needs are not met in third-world countries, whereas they are in first world countries. Not paying debts is a sign of not contributing to the industrialized economy, yet receiving benefits from it. Demographic transition entails explosive population growth, which can be problematic for countries already overcrowded. Globalization creates cultural homogeneity and loss of previous cultures to adapt the new, globalized one.
