A child's primary carer should receive a wage from the government until the child enters primary school or some other form of care. Raising a child is just as much, if not more, work than jobs which traditionally receive economic reward. This wage would likely offset some of the cost of having a child, but it would not make childrearing profitable. People should have children for love, not money because, People could start having children for money, which would cheapen the parental experience.
