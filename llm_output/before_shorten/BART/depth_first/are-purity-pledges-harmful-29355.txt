Purity pledges are harmful. The predominant complaint about purity pledges isn't that they are ineffective in making people's lives better, but that they make people who don't want to take or adhere to them feel bad. Individuals who break their pledge may feel a sense of guilt, an inability to ask for advice, and a fear of accessing sexual health clinics (Santelli, 27530260-4/pdf)). This impedes their ability to engage in safe sex. Most purity pledgers are children who do not understand what they are committing to.
