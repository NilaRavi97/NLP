The risk of deanonymization is at least as high for centralized marketplaces as information is usually stored, as the name suggests, in a central location. This serves to protect the privacy of users against interference, for example by governments. Decentralised marketplaces require ALL users to host/store and distribute content, which may make it easier to deanonymize users as opposed to buyers and sellers just visiting a website.
