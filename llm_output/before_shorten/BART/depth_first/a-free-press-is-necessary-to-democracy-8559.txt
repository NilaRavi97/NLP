A free Press is necessary to democracy. The foundational principle of a free society is that rightful political power comes from the consent of the governed. However, consent is meaningless unless it is informed consent. Citizens should be free to criticise their governors and to hold them to account. They are free to criticize but do not because it is not in their best interests. Criticism itself has limited value in that it is purely negative. Actual change requires positive suggestions also. Critic of others is not as valuable to a society as promoting innovative, evidence-based ideas for fixing problems. Problems must nonetheless be identified before solutions can be created. In particular, the problems must be shared before the solutions will be widely accepted. Solutions should be the goal
